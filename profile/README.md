# over social

> **The dead internet is over.**

Somewhere along the way the social internet stopped feeling social. Feeds filled with content engineered to enrage, bots talking to bots, communities flattened into engagement metrics, and the whole thing steered by algorithms tuned for someone else's quarterly numbers. It got lonelier the more "connected" it claimed to make us. The premise here is that this era is ending — and that what comes after can be built on purpose.

The idea is to put people back at the center: real connections instead of recommended ones, communities that belong to their members instead of to a platform, and conversation shaped by the humans having it rather than by a ranking model deciding what they should see. No algorithmic feed pulling the strings, no swarm of fake accounts, no single owner whose interests quietly override everyone else's.

A return to the simple thing the network was always supposed to be — people, finding each other, for real.
